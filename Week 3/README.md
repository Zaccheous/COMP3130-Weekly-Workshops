## Workshop Overview

This workshop focused on **User Experience (UX) Research** and how UX methods can be used to evaluate a mobile application.  

Our group reviewed the **Duolingo mobile application** and created a survey using **Qualtrics** to gather feedback from users. The purpose of this activity was to explore how usability, accessibility, inclusive design, and visual design affect the overall user experience.

The collected survey data was then analysed to identify common issues and provide recommendations for improving the app.

---

## Objectives

The objectives of this workshop were to:

- Understand the principles of **User Experience (UX) design**
- Apply **user-centred research methods**
- Create a **survey using Qualtrics**
- Collect and analyse user feedback
- Identify **UX improvements for the application**

## Application Reviewed

**Duolingo**

Duolingo is a language-learning mobile application that helps users learn new languages through short interactive lessons, quizzes, and gamified learning activities.

## Part 1 – Survey Creation

A survey was designed using **Qualtrics** to collect user feedback about the Duolingo app.

The survey focused on four UX metrics:

1. **Usability** – How easy it is to perform tasks in the application  
2. **Accessibility** – Whether the app supports users with different abilities or devices  
3. **Inclusive Design** – Whether the application is suitable for diverse users  
4. **Overall Look and Feel** – The visual design and user satisfaction

## Part 2 – Survey Distribution

The survey was shared with other groups during the workshop session.

Participants were given **20 minutes** to explore the Duolingo app and complete the survey. The responses were then collected and analysed to identify common user experiences and challenges.

---

## Results Summary

Over half of the respondents reported that they had previously used Duolingo. However, only about half of the participants who completed the survey were able to successfully navigate to their friends list and add another user. This suggests that the user interface (UI) may not be clear enough and can be confusing, even for returning users.

Several users also reported difficulties when navigating between lessons or attempting to pause their progress during a lesson. In addition, some participants mentioned that the application contains too many tabs and steps to complete simple tasks, which can make navigation more complicated than necessary.

Despite these usability issues, the majority of participants provided positive feedback regarding the visual design of the application. Most respondents rated the aesthetics of Duolingo as good, and a few rated it as average or excellent. Importantly, there were no negative comments about the visual appearance of the app.

Overall, while Duolingo performs well in terms of visual design and aesthetics, improvements could be made to simplify navigation and make key features easier for users to find and access.

---

## Recommendations

Based on the survey results, the following improvements are suggested:

- Simplify navigation within the application  
- Reduce the number of steps required to complete simple tasks  
- Improve the visibility of features such as the **friends list**  
- Make it easier for users to switch between lessons or pause their progress  

Implementing these improvements could enhance usability while maintaining the strong visual design of the application.
